

Fictional cities are often obvious stand-ins for real life cities. They have a different name, different landmarks and a different population, but the look, conventions and society at large are the same. This gives the setting more flavor than just calling it city of adventure.

Where names or street numbers are obscured, or not obscured at all, but accurately based on real locations, real place background applies.

See also hollywood atlas, big applesauce and fantasy counterpart culture. Occasionally involves istanbul not constantinople. Contrast with city with no name, where the hell is springfield? and canada does not exist when the city's location is deliberately concealed or seems to contradict itself.
